First year:

Every year on a specific date, people interested in service design and a design-based approach to problem solving and creativity meet all over the globe. With the slogan being '48 hours to change the world.' Teams have 48 hours to develop brand new services on a chosen by a shared theme, within that time frame. On the last day,ideas are published online. The first year I took part (2018), my team focused on community. We went out and asked the public about their community and how they participate within it. Our final design was a 'community street' where local people would occasionally open their door for the day/ or weekend and share their skills with the community.

Second year:

The second year that I took part (2019), I joined the organising team and in charge of the liaison with the other jam cities. The theme that year was 'blue' and, after some brainstorming, we focused on 'music' as blue made us think of the blues. When we went out and questioned people about their relationship with music we noticed a big difference in the way elderly and younger generations accessed music. We wanted to create a common way for all generations to access/ discover music, and our final idea was a music library. It would work in the same way as a conventional library, but would only offer music and instruments. We also conceived a mobile music library for anyone with accessibility issues and to reach out to schools in smaller towns with fewer resources.
