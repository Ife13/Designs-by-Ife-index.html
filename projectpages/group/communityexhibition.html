The Brief for this project was to work as a group to design a specific 'co-living' shared area which would be in the same building as our individual co-units. My group worked on the 'social' area, we recognised that each individual is quite different, so we wanted to create a space where a variety of people can merge. We had a lecture on the psychology of personal space, which spoke about how a certain distance you keep from another person can show what kind of relationship you have with them/how comfortable you are around them. Through research, we discovered, personality wise, people are either 'introverts, ambiverts or extroverts' and there are three different types of body language 'looker, listener or toucher'. As a social space is usually somewhere where people stay/ sit for a while we wanted the seating to reflect the different types of individuals we had discovered in our research. The seating was designed in a way that forced people to sit a certain distance from each other/ in a certain way according to the social distance it is representing. 

After completing our CO-unit and CO-mmunity projects we decided, as a class, to present our work in an exhibition. We split ourselves into groups; sponsorships, promotion, exhibition design, building and vinyl cutting (which I was a part of). The exhibition took place an open space near the reception of the art college and had a good turn out with; friends, family, students and lecturers.
